# Founder Hooks Prompt Pack

The first one or two lines of a LinkedIn post decide whether anyone reads the rest. Only those lines show before the "see more" cut, so the hook is the whole game. This pack gives you a master AI prompt plus 12 proven hook formulas, each with an example, to write hooks that stop your ideal buyer from scrolling.

Made by [PromiseClick](https://www.promiseclick.com). Full guide and more playbooks: [promiseclick.com/resources/playbooks](https://www.promiseclick.com/resources/playbooks).

## How to use this pack

- **With AI:** copy the master prompt below into ChatGPT or Claude, fill in the brackets, and run it. Generate 10, keep 2.
- **Without AI:** use the formulas directly. Each is a fill-in-the-blank template with an example.
- **Always test line 1.** If it does not make sense out of context, it will not stop the scroll.

## The master prompt

Paste this into ChatGPT or Claude and fill in the brackets:

```
You are a B2B LinkedIn ghostwriter for a founder.

Context:
- Who I help: [your ICP, e.g. Heads of Product at Series A-B SaaS]
- What I help them do: [the outcome you create]
- My point of view: [a belief you hold that not everyone agrees with]
- Topic of this post: [the topic]

Write 10 scroll-stopping LinkedIn hooks (the first 1-2 lines only) for a post on that topic.

Rules:
- Speak directly to my ICP and their problem, not a general audience.
- Make line 1 short, specific, and concrete. No throat-clearing like "In today's world".
- Vary the angles across the 10: contrarian, mistake, result, number, callout, story, bold claim, and question.
- Keep each hook under 12 words where possible.
- No hashtags, no emojis.

Return a numbered list and label the angle of each hook.
```

## 12 hook formulas

Each formula is a starting line. Swap in your specifics and keep it concrete.

### 1. Contrarian
**Formula:** Everyone says [common advice]. It is wrong.
**Example:** Everyone says post daily. That is why your reach is dropping.

### 2. Mistake / confession
**Formula:** I wasted [time or money] doing [thing]. Here is what I would do instead.
**Example:** I spent a year posting tips nobody asked for. Here is what I would do instead.

### 3. Result / proof
**Formula:** We took [metric] from [X] to [Y] in [time]. Here is how.
**Example:** We took reply rates from 4% to 30% in 60 days. Here is how.

### 4. Number / listicle
**Formula:** [N] [things] that [outcome]. Most people skip number [k].
**Example:** 5 LinkedIn posts that book calls. Most founders skip number 3.

### 5. Callout
**Formula:** If you are a [ICP] doing [thing], stop.
**Example:** If you are a founder posting for reach, stop.

### 6. Story
**Formula:** [Time marker], [a specific scene].
**Example:** Last Tuesday a prospect replied to a post I almost did not publish.

### 7. Bold claim
**Formula:** [Common thing] is dead. Here is what replaced it.
**Example:** Cold outreach is dead. Here is what replaced it.

### 8. Question
**Formula:** Why do [ICP] keep [painful pattern]?
**Example:** Why do great founders keep getting ignored on LinkedIn?

### 9. Before / after
**Formula:** I went from [bad state] to [good state]. One thing changed.
**Example:** I went from 200 to 100k views a post. One thing changed.

### 10. Myth-bust
**Formula:** The biggest myth about [topic]: [the myth].
**Example:** The biggest myth about LinkedIn: more followers means more pipeline.

### 11. Cost / stakes
**Formula:** [Mistake] is quietly costing you [tangible cost].
**Example:** A vague headline is quietly costing you half your profile visits.

### 12. Steal this
**Formula:** Steal my [asset] for [outcome].
**Example:** Steal my 5-line DM that books calls without a pitch.

## Tips that make hooks land

- The first 1-2 lines are all that show before "see more". Front-load the tension.
- Specific beats clever. A real number or a named situation outperforms wordplay.
- One idea per hook. If it needs a comma to survive, it is two hooks.
