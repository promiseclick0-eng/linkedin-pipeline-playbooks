## 2. Apply the red-flag gate

Pause a provider before scoring if any answer is yes and the risk cannot be resolved in writing.

- [ ] Results cannot be connected to a real context, baseline, time frame, or approved customer.
- [ ] The provider guarantees reach, replies, meetings, or revenue without defensible conditions.
- [ ] Account access, data ownership, asset ownership, or handoff is unclear.
- [ ] The workflow relies on unsafe or prohibited automation.
- [ ] Customer names, quotes, or metrics appear to be used without permission.
- [ ] The provider recommends a package before understanding the ICP, offer, and bottleneck.
- [ ] Reporting emphasizes surface engagement but cannot explain qualified conversations or learning.
- [ ] The work would publish claims or messages without an agreed human approval step.
- [ ] Exit terms would leave the team without its data, content, documentation, or accounts.

## 3. Score the evidence

Use a 1 to 5 score:

- **1:** no useful evidence or clear mismatch
- **2:** weak evidence with major gaps
- **3:** acceptable evidence with manageable gaps
- **4:** strong evidence and clear fit
- **5:** exceptional evidence with low uncertainty

Formula:

```text
Weighted points = category weight x score / 5
```

## 4. Use the category checks

### ICP and strategic diagnosis, 20 points

- Do they understand the buyer, buying role, offer, and expensive problem?
- Can they identify the current bottleneck before prescribing a tactic?
- Can they explain what they would not do?
- Do they connect profile, content, conversations, and pipeline?

### LinkedIn specialization, 15 points

- Is their recent work specific to LinkedIn?
- Does it match the service you need: positioning, content, outreach, paid, or a complete system?
- Have they worked with a similar market, sales motion, or founder constraint?
- Can they explain current platform uncertainty without pretending to know a secret algorithm?

### Proof quality, 15 points

- Does each example include context, baseline, work, time frame, and outcome?
- Are customer identity and permission clear?
- Are business outcomes separated from vanity metrics?
- Can they explain weak results and what changed afterward?

### Operating system, 15 points

- Who researches, writes, approves, publishes, replies, and records outcomes?
- What access is required and how is it protected?
- What is the approval cadence?
- What happens when the founder is unavailable?
- What documentation and assets remain after the engagement?

### Measurement and learning, 10 points

- Which leading and lagging indicators matter?
- How are qualified conversations identified?
- How are attribution limits explained?
- What decision does each report help the team make?

### Founder voice and brand safety, 10 points

- How is the founder's real judgment captured?
- How are claims, customer details, and permissions verified?
- Is AI used with review rather than hidden automation?
- Can the founder reject work without slowing the whole system?

### Execution quality, 10 points

- Are samples clear, specific, accurate, and easy to scan?
- Does the work sound like the client or the agency template?
- Are messages relevant and proportionate to the relationship?
- Does the provider demonstrate its own standard publicly?

### Commercial fit, 5 points

- Do scope, capacity, price, and commitment fit the team?
- Are exclusions and additional costs explicit?
- Is there a credible first-30-day plan?
- Are renewal and exit terms clear?

## 5. Ask every provider the same questions

1. What must you diagnose before recommending a scope?
2. Which part of our current system would you leave unchanged at first?
3. Show the closest relevant example and explain the baseline, work, time frame, and outcome.
4. Which result are you most confident you can influence, and which can you not promise?
5. Who owns each step from research to reporting?
6. How do you preserve founder voice and verify claims?
7. How do you identify a qualified conversation?
8. What platform or account risks should we understand?
9. What will our team own and know when the engagement ends?
10. What would make us a poor fit for you?

## 6. Run reference checks

Ask a recent client:

- What did the provider diagnose correctly?
- What required more internal work than expected?
- How close were the samples and sales process to delivery reality?
- How did the team handle weak performance or disagreement?
- What did you own and understand when the engagement ended?
- Would you choose the provider again for the same problem?

Record the date, relationship, permission, and exact notes. Do not treat an anonymous testimonial as a reference call.
