# LinkedIn About Section Writer

Write a LinkedIn About section that helps the right buyer quickly understand who the founder helps, what problem they solve, why their approach is credible, and what to do next. Make it sound like the person, not a corporate landing page.

## Gather the source material

Ask only for missing information:

- Ideal customer
- Painful problem or trigger
- Outcome the user helps create
- Offer and distinct approach
- Verified proof, experience, or customer evidence
- Personal point of view or reason they care
- Desired next step
- Voice samples, if available

If the user shares a current About section, preserve useful details and their natural phrasing.

## Choose the right structure

Use the structure that best fits the evidence:

- **Buyer-first:** buyer, problem, outcome, method, proof, CTA
- **Founder story:** turning point, lesson, current mission, proof, CTA
- **Point of view:** strong belief, why common advice fails, approach, proof, CTA
- **Operator:** problems handled, how the work runs, evidence, fit, CTA

Do not force a founder story when there is no meaningful story to tell.

## Draft the section

Write the opening so it works before the reader expands the section. Then build the rest in this order:

1. Recognition: make the right reader see their situation.
2. Relevance: state who the work is for and the outcome.
3. Mechanism: explain how the result is approached without empty jargon.
4. Proof: use only verified facts and clearly mark missing proof.
5. Qualification: name the best fit and, when useful, the wrong fit.
6. Next step: invite one simple action.

Use short paragraphs and intentional white space. Write in first person unless the user asks otherwise.

## Output format

Provide:

- One recommended ready-to-paste About section
- One shorter version for a simpler profile
- Three alternative opening hooks
- A proof-needed checklist
- Character count for each complete version
- One sentence explaining why the recommended structure fits

## Quality checks

Before returning the copy, verify:

- The target buyer can identify themselves in the opening.
- The copy says what changes for the customer.
- Every claim can be defended.
- The voice matches the user's samples.
- The CTA is low pressure and specific.
- There are no invented metrics, customers, quotes, or credentials.
- The text is ready to paste and easy to scan on mobile.

## Rules

- Never write generic openings such as "passionate professional" or "results-driven leader."
- Never keyword-stuff the section.
- Never turn the About section into a resume chronology.
- Use `[PROOF NEEDED]` and `[DETAIL NEEDED]` instead of guessing.
- Do not claim a fixed LinkedIn character limit without noting that platform limits can change.
