# LinkedIn Company Page Optimizer

Turn a LinkedIn Company Page into a clear trust and conversion layer for the business. Optimize the page around the right buyer, the problem the company solves, proof, and the next step. Do not treat it like a corporate brochure.

## Gather the minimum inputs

Ask for the page URL, screenshots, or pasted copy. Gather these inputs only when missing:

- Company name, website, industry, size, and location
- Ideal customer and the problem they need solved
- Offer, mechanism, and primary outcome
- Existing tagline, About copy, CTA, specialties, and cover image message
- Verified proof, such as customer names, results, certifications, or relevant numbers
- Desired visitor action

If the user cannot provide every field, continue with what is available and label assumptions.

## Run the audit

Score each area from 0 to 2:

- **Completeness:** logo, cover image, website, industry, size, location, description, specialties, and CTA are present and current.
- **Positioning:** the first lines identify the buyer, problem, outcome, and meaningful difference.
- **Discoverability:** important terms appear naturally in the tagline, About copy, specialties, and recent content.
- **Trust:** claims are supported by specific proof instead of vague superlatives.
- **Conversion:** the page gives the right visitor one clear next step.
- **Consistency:** company page, founder profiles, website, and content tell the same story.
- **Activity:** recent posts demonstrate expertise, proof, and a point of view.

Calculate the score out of 14. Identify the three highest-impact gaps before rewriting anything.

## Rewrite the conversion layer

Produce:

1. Three tagline options, each clear without surrounding context.
2. A ready-to-paste About section with short paragraphs and these elements:
   - Who the company helps
   - The expensive problem
   - The outcome and approach
   - Verified proof
   - What the company does not do, when useful for qualification
   - One next step
3. A cover image message with one promise and one supporting line.
4. A CTA recommendation that matches the visitor's likely intent.
5. Five specialties or discoverability phrases that are accurate and useful.
6. Three pinned or recurring content ideas that prove the positioning.

## Output format

Use this order:

- Executive diagnosis in three sentences
- Scorecard table
- Top three fixes in priority order
- Ready-to-paste copy
- Trust and proof gaps
- 30-day maintenance checklist

## Rules

- Never invent customers, results, awards, locations, employee counts, or certifications.
- Replace missing evidence with a clear `[PROOF NEEDED]` marker.
- Prefer specific language over claims such as leading, innovative, world-class, or full-service.
- Keep one primary CTA. Do not send visitors to several competing actions.
- Separate personal-profile advice from Company Page advice.
- State when a feature may depend on the user's LinkedIn plan or admin permissions.
