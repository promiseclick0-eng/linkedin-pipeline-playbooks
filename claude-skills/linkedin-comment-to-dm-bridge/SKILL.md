# LinkedIn Comment to DM Bridge

Move a real public interaction into a private conversation only when the transition helps the other person. Preserve the context. Do not use a comment as permission to pitch.

## Gather the interaction

Ask for:

- Original post or a faithful summary
- The prospect's exact comment
- The user's exact public reply
- Any previous relationship or DMs
- The user's offer and ICP, for relevance checks only
- The desired outcome

If exact wording is unavailable, label the result as a general template instead of pretending it is personalized.

## Read the signal

Classify the interaction:

- **Acknowledge only:** polite reaction, emoji, generic agreement, or no meaningful opening
- **Continue publicly:** a useful question or discussion that benefits the wider thread
- **Permission to DM:** explicit request for a resource, example, detail, introduction, or private context
- **Commercial signal:** clear problem, active project, request for help, or buying question

Do not move to DMs for an acknowledge-only signal. Explain why and suggest a useful public reply.

## Choose the bridge

- **Resource bridge:** deliver the requested item and state why it fits.
- **Detail bridge:** continue a nuanced point that would clutter or expose the public thread.
- **Question bridge:** ask one focused question directly tied to what they said.
- **Permission bridge:** ask publicly before sending private information.
- **Commercial bridge:** acknowledge the stated problem, clarify it, and delay any call request until there is mutual fit.

## Write the sequence

For a valid DM transition, produce:

1. Optional public bridge reply
2. First DM that references the exact interaction
3. One natural question that is easy to answer
4. Two next-message branches:
   - They engage with detail
   - They give a short or hesitant response
5. A stop rule explaining when not to follow up

Keep each message focused on one job. Do not jump from public interaction to a calendar link.

## Output format

- Signal classification and evidence
- Recommended channel: public, DM, or stop
- Ready-to-send message
- Alternative message
- Branches and timing
- Risk check for assumptions, pressure, or privacy

## Rules

- Never turn a like or generic comment into a buying signal.
- Never claim the recipient asked for something they did not ask for.
- Never copy private details into a public reply.
- Never pitch in the first DM.
- Never use fake familiarity or generic flattery.
- Stop after a clear no, no relevant response, or repeated silence.
