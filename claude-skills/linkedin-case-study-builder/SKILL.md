# LinkedIn Case Study Builder

Turn raw delivery evidence into a useful customer story that demonstrates judgment, process, and outcomes. Protect credibility and client confidentiality. A smaller verified result is stronger than an impressive invented one.

## Build the evidence ledger

Gather and classify:

- Client identity and permission status
- Starting situation
- Business problem and consequences
- Constraints
- Work completed
- Key decision or insight
- Timeline
- Baseline and outcome metrics
- Client quotes with their exact source
- What cannot be disclosed
- Desired reader and next step

Create three lists before writing:

- **Verified:** supported by supplied evidence
- **Needs confirmation:** plausible but not yet supported
- **Private:** excluded or anonymized

Do not draft until the key outcome and permission status are clear. If they are unavailable, create an anonymized process story and label it accordingly.

## Choose the right case study angle

- **Before and after:** use when baseline and outcome are verified.
- **Decision story:** use when the strongest value was a strategic choice.
- **Constraint story:** use when a difficult limit shaped the solution.
- **Process story:** use when metrics are unavailable but the method is useful.
- **Customer voice:** use when an approved testimonial carries the proof.

## Build the core narrative

Use this sequence:

1. Specific result or tension
2. Relevant starting situation
3. The real diagnosis
4. What changed and why
5. Verified outcome
6. Transferable lesson
7. Low-pressure next step

Focus on the customer's transformation, not the provider's self-praise.

## Produce the proof package

Create:

- One full LinkedIn case study post
- One short proof post
- One Featured section title and description
- Three proof snippets for profile, proposal, or DM use
- One carousel outline
- One evidence ledger showing the source behind every metric and quote
- A permission checklist before publication

## Rules

- Never invent or round up metrics.
- Never combine results from different customers.
- Never write a quote the client did not say.
- Never expose a client, employee, or commercial detail without permission.
- Use `[CONFIRM]` for unsupported claims and `[ANONYMIZED]` for hidden identities.
- Distinguish correlation from causation.
- State the measurement window and baseline when reporting change.
- Remove vanity metrics when they do not support the business outcome.
